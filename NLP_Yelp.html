3. Description of the analysis to be conducted
For this dataset, several NLP techniques were applied to uncover valuable insights from the reviews. One approach was sentiment analysis, which allowed us to classify reviews into positive, neutral, or negative categories based on the text. This helped correlate sentiment with star ratings and identify discrepancies, such as users leaving low-star reviews despite using generally positive language, or vice versa.


Additionally, topic modeling using Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was performed on two subsets of the datahigh and low ratings. This approach identified common themes discussed in each subset, such as customer service, food quality, or delivery issues. By separating the reviews into these categories, we could compare the topics associated with high ratings versus those with low ratings, providing deeper insights into what drives customer satisfaction or dissatisfaction.


Combining sentiment analysis and topic modeling provided a comprehensive understanding of customer preferences, pain points, and the key drivers of both positive and negative experiences.

def absa_sentiment(review, aspect):
    inputs = absa_tokenizer(f"[CLS] {review} [SEP] {aspect} [SEP]", return_tensors="pt")

    return dict(zip(["negative", "neutral", "positive"], probs))

I employed three sentiment analysis methods: FLAIR, VADER, Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA).


FLAIR: Compared to the star ratings, FLAIR performed exceptionally well, achieving an accuracy of over 94%. Since FLAIR is pre-trained on datasets that include movie and product reviews, it is highly suited for sentiment analysis in this context. Its ability to align closely with the star ratings makes it a strong method for identifying overall sentiment in the reviews.


VADER: Unlike FLAIR, VADER categorizes sentiment into three distinct classes: positive, neutral, and negative. This helps clarify the overall tone of the reviews, especially in cases where mixed sentiments are present. However, VADER did not perform as well as anticipated. A significant number of reviews were classified as neutral, despite containing obvious positive or negative language. This suggests that VADER may struggle with nuanced or complex sentiments in this dataset


Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA): ABSA allows for the evaluation of sentiments regarding specific aspects of a review, which is useful when opinions differ across attributes like food or service. I focused on two commonly mentioned aspects: 'food' and 'service' since they appear frequently in the dataset. Due to computation time and memory limitations, I analyzed only a sample of 1,000 reviews. While this was sufficient to showcase the models functionality, a larger sample would provide more comprehensive insights.
In terms of results,  after a closer evaluation of individual results, ABSA not only captured the positive and negative tones effectively and but also accurately distinguish between sentiments toward different aspects. For example, several reviews expressed positive sentiment toward the food but negative sentiment toward the service, and ABSA was able to capture this distinction effectively. This shows that the model can correctly identify aspect-specific sentiments, even though there is room for further refinement in handling more complex reviews.


All in all, the analysis provides a solid foundation for understanding how each model handles sentiment in relation to the star ratings and specific review aspects.
